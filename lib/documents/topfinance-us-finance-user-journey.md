# TopFinancs US Finance User Journey - Color and UI Recommendations

## 1. The Action Button (CTA)

Currently, your "Get Credit Card" button is the same blue as the previous survey buttons. This is a missed opportunity. To increase effectiveness, the final CTA should **pop** against everything else.

- **Suggested Color:** **Vibrant Orange (#FF8C00)** or **Soft Golden Yellow (#FFD700)**.
  - **Why:** Orange creates a sense of urgency and activity without the "stop" or "danger" connotation of red. It stands out significantly against the blue brand color.
- **Font Color:** **Deep Navy or Black**.
  - **Why:** High contrast is essential for readability. Dark text on a bright warm-colored button is easier to read than white.

## 2. The Survey Buttons (Question Phase)

These should feel like progress, not a commitment. The blue is fine, but it can be softened to make the final "Get" button feel more important.

- **Suggested Color:** **Royal Blue (#0056b3)** for the button and **White** for the font.
- **Alternative:** Use an **Outline (Ghost) Button** for "No" or "Other" and a **Solid Blue** for "Yes" or specific benefits. This guides the user's eye toward the positive path.

## 3. The Font and Readability

- **Font Choice:** Use a clean, modern Sans-Serif (like **Inter**, **Roboto**, or **Open Sans**).
- **Font Weight:** Bold the questions, but keep the "To continue, it will be necessary to watch a short ad" text in a lighter weight and slightly smaller size.
- **Font Color on White Background:** Use a **Dark Grey (#333333)** rather than pure black. It reduces eye strain and looks more modern.

### A Quick Tip on Trust

Since the user is required to watch an ad before the final step, they may feel a "bait and switch" moment. To keep them engaged, ensure the **"Get Credit Card"** button looks significantly more "premium" (maybe with a subtle drop shadow or rounded corners) than the survey buttons to signal that the reward is worth the ad.
